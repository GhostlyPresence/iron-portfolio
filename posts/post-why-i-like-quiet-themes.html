Why I Like Quiet Themes

On choosing soft colors and gentle contrasts for most of the work I do.

I spend a lot of hours in front of screens, so I care about how they feel. Loud themes are fun for a moment, but over time I gravitate toward softer palettes — greens, warm neutrals, and subtle contrast that lets the content breathe.

Quiet themes keep my focus on the text and the structure instead of the chrome around them. They make it easier to stay in flow, the same way reading in a calm room feels different from trying to read in a crowded train station.

This portfolio borrows that idea: a small, steady visual language that stays out of the way while the work does the talking.
